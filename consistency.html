Usability Evaluation of Georgia State University

Jane, Jeffrey, and Kenna

COGS 187A

4. Consistency and Standards

"Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing. Follow platform conventions."
– Jakob Nielsen



Top navigation drop down menus only appear for the first 3 titles, skip the next 3, and again have a drop down for the last title. After hovering over the first couple titles, one would expect that they all have drop down menus. Although all navigation bar elements are present, there is inconsistency among which elements are underlined in red and which are not. The inconsistency makes the website unorganized and messy.


The search bar gives the option of searching through "Georgia State" or "Directory." Itâ€™s unclear as to what either of these mean, and I am still unsure as to the differences between them. When using the search bar, it leads to Google half the time, and to another portion of the GSU website the other half. This is very misleading and users generally want to be lead to a search within the same website, rather than being lead to the Google search screen.
