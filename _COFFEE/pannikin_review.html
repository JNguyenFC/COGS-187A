Tess B.

Pannikin is my favorite coffee shop in the history of coffee shops. Not only is is a fun place to hang out, enjoy coffee, and shoot the breeze; but it has a cute little gift shop too. Also, Pannikin's baked goods are awesome enough to have come from your mom/aunt/grandma who can bake.

My favorite drinks are hot chocolate and the cafe latte.

Can't beat the repurposed train station ambiance, and there are both indoor and outdoor seating. Heck, you could probably even bring a blanket and sit on the lawn if you wanted.

Dawn H.

I love Pannikin. It has a great atmosphere with lots of outdoor seating, its pastries are delicious, and so is the rest of the food. My favorite drink is the Mexican hot chocolate. This past visit, I got a Greek salad with a bowl of chili. The salad came with a pesto-flavored dressing that was delicious, even if I did accidentally spill almost all of it, making a soup out of my salad. The chili was good, not the best, but good. My husband got the curry chicken sandwich and enjoyed it. My favorite thing about Pannikin, though is that every time I see it, there are lots of people outside lounging in the sun and enjoying their food, drinks, and good company.

Great place to enjoy a long breakfast. This place has the Encinitas beach town vibe but pulls it off without being too hipster. Coffee is roasted on site and pastries are delicious as well. I'd recommend the Bandiera bagel (bagel w/ cream cheese, sun dried tomatoes, and pesto) with a Keith Richards (triple shot mocha). Nice way to start a lazy weekend.

Tiffany A.

Still in love with this place.

This quaint old train station is always calling my name. I'm not in the area enough to frequent it often, but every once in a while I'll go out of my way to stop by, step back and enjoy the simplicity of this place.

Great place if you're in the neighborhood for a light snack, drink or meal.

They also sell teas and random candies and trinkets.

You'll see tons of people from around the neighborhood, bicyclist making a stop by and those who seek this place out. I'm not surprised that it can get busy throughout the day... the place is charming!

Only con is the parking lot is small and most of the time I've ended up parking in the residential area.

Dawn H.

Quaint & rustic coffee shop with a touch of history.

The prices are lower than Starbucks and their coffee is actually good! Staff is friendly.

Parking is in the back or on the street behind/next do Pannikin.

If you come here in the wintertime, make sure you bundle up since the coffeeshop opens up to the outside and it gets cold!
