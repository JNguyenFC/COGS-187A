Lucia N.

So excited that my work moved down the street from this place! Now I have to monitor my weekly coffee budget haha. High quality coffee at awesome prices. I would take this over Starbucks any day. Storefront doesn't say Zumbar (I wonder why), it only says "Coffee Shop". It's in a small strip mall by the Sorrento Valley Coaster Station. Easy to miss if you didn't know it was there.

Their drinks are traditional here. A Macchiato (which the barista and owner nicely schooled me about) is not like Starbucks. It's two shots of espresso with a lil bit of foam on top, served in a small cup. Latte's are delicious. French press is the way to go if you're dining in . They give you the whole french press at your table so it's enough for two cups of coffee.

Leigh K.

I have been a fan of Zumbar long before I ever stepped foot into this storefront.

My brother and I have coffee competitions. We find local roasters and buy bags and then have coffee tastings to see who wins. I discovered that Zumbar not only had a shop, but also roasted its own beans. Score. So, I bought some. Then I bought more. And more. I've tried a few different roasts and have loved them all. I've ordered online and I've also purchased beans at a local gelato shop that endorses Zumbar coffee beans.

I have never been into the actual storefront simply because I never find myself in Sorrento Valley, and if/when I am in the area, it's closed. I'm off work at 5:00... they close at 5:00. I haven't had an excuse to stop in for a latte until today, when I had to drop a friend at the airport suuuuper early and decided I would splurge and go in.

They were friendly, helpful, made a perfect soy latte, helped me select a new roast to buy, and gave me two free drink coupons to use at their new store that opens today in Cardiff. Plus, when they asked if I wanted the coffee beans ground and I answered, "And ruin them?" the guy told me that was the best answer he'd ever heard.

I will definitely be hitting up the Cardiff location. Great local coffee... beats out the others in San Diego for sure.

Alan T.

Just saying, don't go to this place if you liked mixed coffee drinks. This place is much more traditional (and hipster).

My boss recommended this place for weeks before we made a trip there. I was not much of a coffee person to begin with so I did not know what to expect. I like to order the standard Latte there and it was delicious. The first thing was that the coffee was not scolding hot, you can drink it immediately - a big plus in my book. Second was the quality of the beans. They roast their own beans in-house so it is extremely fresh and extremely smooth. I love it so much that I have bought their Hummingbird whole beans myself!
