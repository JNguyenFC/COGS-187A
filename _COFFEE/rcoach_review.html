Mick G.

Fresh pour single cup coffee on the sidewalk?

Yes please.

There are a few good components to good coffee.

Good beans. Done.

Good roast. Done, and varied. From light to dark, they have your needs met.

Good process. Fresh pour single cone filter deliciousness.

Good maker. The above components mean almost nothing without someone who really really really loves what they are doing and what they are making.

Here this is achieved. Get coffee here. It is DELICIOUS.

Jessica H.

These women are passionate about the coffee business. They take pride in their product and it shows. A steady stream of regulars visit them and darned if they don't know their standard order, their dogs' name, and their kids. It's a pride of ownership that I love to see.

I usually get their Sunrise on 30th - an orange juice vanilla milk blend that's sweet without being cloying. I'm also a fan of their abelskeivers. So delicious!

Mariela D.

OH MY GOD YES.

Being new to San Diego and born and raised in SF, I pretty much squealed aloud when I discovered Roast Coach. They offer the individual pour-over style of San Francisco's Philz Coffee without the pretentiousness. The owners, also from SF, are rad and greet every customer as if they're a longtime friend.

I've had Southern Hospitality which is like a mint mojito iced coffee. So refreshing and lightly sweet. Perfect for a warm day, which is like 95% of days in San Diego. I have yet to try their Horchata latte, but that's next on my list!

They carry an assortment of pastries, and on the weekends, you can even snag these delectable Swedish pancake balls (there's a joke in here somewhere).

Thank you, Roast Coach, for making me miss home a little less. I'm glad you're here.

Annie D.

As soon as I heard about Roast Coach, I went out and had to try it. It is a coffee bar parked in from of Sea Rocket in North Park. The women running the 'coach' were very friendly. I had a pour over for $3. It was definitely good coffee, especially for San Diego. It may in fact be the best coffee I have had in San Diego (with the exception of the coffee I make in my own home, of course). Roast Coach definitely offers the kind of coffee for coffee-snobs, not for the average person. It is truly about the coffee for them, not about snacks or a place to relax and hang out with a friend. If high quality coffee is what you want, then try out Roast Coach. These people know what they are doing and they know how to treat customers. If you are not so much a coffee-snob, then perhaps you should save yourself the trouble and by cheaper coffee in one of the many cozy coffee houses in the area.
