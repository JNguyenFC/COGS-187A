Christopher W.

This coffee shop located in North Park contains a bunch of hipsters, college kids, etc. and it's to be expected.It definitely is one of the more infamous coffee shops in San Diego and with good reason.

With plentiful leather furniture and diverse works of art throughout the establishment it definitely sets a unique tone that has your mind jumping from place to place. While I am no coffee connoisseur, everything I've had from there I've enjoyed and have not been disappointed.

My only recommendation to them is to turn the friggin' air conditioner on. With all the body heat in there with computers and all the other equipment generating heat, it gets hot as hell up in there. The place is open 24 hours a day so that is a huge plus for late night owls such as myself where much is accomplished with the sun down. Check'em out and have a cup of java!

Stephanie C.

If you are looking for somewhere to catch up with friends, get work done, or just hang out, this is the PERFECT place to do so.

I asked the barista to make his favorite drink, and he handed me a "Caramel Freeze," which was his own off-the-menu creation. Okay maybe it was just a twist on an existing drink, but YUM! It was absolutely delicious and FULL of flavor. (Usually I find that frozen drinks taste more like ice than flavor.) Plus, all their drink names sound so fun, like Strawberry Dream and Arctic Squirrel. I will definitely start to tackle the entire drink menu as I return here again and again.

The environment is optimal for productivity and relaxation. There is plenty of seating, power outlets, and space at this location, and when I went, it wasn't super crowded or noisy. The only drawback was that the WiFi stopped working for me and my friends after awhile... but I'm sure it doesn't happen all the time. The decor is fun and quirky, and overall the place feels like home.
