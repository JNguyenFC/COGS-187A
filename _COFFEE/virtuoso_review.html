Matthew S.

One of the coffee roasters of San Diego. Their storefront is a large warehouse space with a few tables and a welcoming atmosphere. Pre-roasted beans are in sacks all over the place and their newly roasted beans are in a large wall for sale.

This place gets five stars because its the tied for the best single shot of espresso I have had in San Diego. Served up with the customary glass of sparkling water their shots are complex and extremely well pulled. They know what they are doing here and love the coffee.

Each drink you order feels like its made specifically for you, without rush. When they ask how your drink was there is often a genuine attention to the answer you give. Pablo, the barista last time I went, epitomizes this sense of care and personal attention. He was great to talk about coffee with.

I like coming here on a weekend and sitting for a couple hours and enjoying a consistently delicious drink. My only wish is that it was closer to where I lived!

Carmen V.

Where East Village meets Barrio Logan, there you find that huger corner building all brick layered with an industrial outskirts of London appeal on the outside, and a warm coffee republic from some exotic land in the inside.

I knew Café Virtuoso from drinking their coffees at various farmers markets and events...I guess visiting it the same day as Café Moto is not fair for them. I am very much into design and even though Virtuoso is neat and sparkling bright and you see the coffee roasting in action...I was too soaked and impressed with the interior design done their closest competitor (and possible friends!).

Still, the coffee at Virtuoso is wonderful as is to watch the roasting process in that attractive bright yellow machinery. When I arrived, there was some cupping going on, and of course I arrived late, but I saw in their website that most Fridays they do have the coffee tasting.

There are also several tables inside and outside, all calling your name. Very quiet and...aromatic!

Abbey M.

Ive been a barista for almost 2 years now..im very passionate about coffee and take pride in knowing how to create good legit coffee (not no starbucks crap) and being the coffee snob that i am..i can honestly say this place has the BEST COFFEE, EVER. Love the staff, love the coffee, love their products, love love love. I live in the IB area, and I always make the drive to Virtuoso. It's worth it. I learned how to be a better barista thanks to Rigo..great guy and really knows his stuff. Pablo is fantastic at making drinks, he's there every Sunday and always remembers what I order and is always willing to make suggestions when I want to try something new. I only have great things to say about this place and the people that work here. LOVE IT ALL! If I could give more than 5 stars I would!
