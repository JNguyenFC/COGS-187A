Usability Evaluation of Georgia State University

Jane, Jeffrey, and Kenna

COGS 187A

10. Help and Documentation

"Even though it is better if the system can be used without documentation, it may be necessary to provide help and documentation. Any such information should be easy to search, focused on the user's task, list concrete steps to be carried out, and not be too large."
– Jakob Nielsen


There are two links on this website titled "Help Center," both under different categories, but that lead to the same page. One of the links is under the "Students" menu, and the other in "Faculty & Staff." Since they are listed under different navigation menus, one may think that there are two different help centers.


When going to the “Help Center,” they do have a FAQ and a submission request. With their submission request, they require that ALL the fields be filled out, even an alternative email, which I think is a bit ridiculous since they also ask for your phone number and your main email address.


On the “Paws” (Panther Access to Web Services) page, when clicking on the “Information System Ethics Policy,” it actually takes you to a page that is not found. All it says is: “Page Not Found” and a GSU search directory right below it. If I put in the search bar, “GSU ethics policy,” nothing comes up even remotely close to what the links should be. Although it’s a minor problem on their website, I think they should keep up to date in relation to what links work and what links don’t work on their anymore.
