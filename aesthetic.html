Usability Evaluation of Georgia State University

Jane, Jeffrey, and Kenna

COGS 187A

8. Aesthetic and Minimalist Design

"Dialogues should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed. Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility."
– Jakob Nielsen


The drop down menus in the top of the screen take up large amounts of other parts of the screen when hovered over. This obscures large portions of the screen, and makes other information unavailable. Users have to remove their mouse from the menu they’ve hovered over in order to recall information from the rest of the screen during this action.



On the home page near the bottom is a section dedicated solely to “What others say about Georgia University.” This is an unnecessary field for the homepage and takes up more space than it's worth for such important real estate. The footer also takes up an extensive amount of unnecessary space, giving it the appearance of just another hover bar with similar information to the navigation at the top of the page.



This drop-down menu displays two sub categories under "Legal Services", but splits the divider up into three portions. Rather than having the hover display split into two sections, it’s split into three with only two that are filled. This is an inefficient design, and makes the menu unbalanced. It would make more sense to split it into two equal sections, rather than three uneven sections.



Going to the “Admissions” page from the navigation bar, we see a list of information graphics. The last information graphic spills over into the footer and compromises the overall design. This is inconsistent with how the footer is used with respect to the rest of the website. In addition, there is large blue space which isn't fully utilized in the footer.
