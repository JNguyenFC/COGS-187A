Usability Evaluation of Georgia State University

Jane, Jeffrey, and Kenna

COGS 187A

7. Flexibility and Efficiency of Use

"Accelerators -- unseen by the novice user -- may often speed up the interaction for the expert user such that the system can cater to both inexperienced and experienced users. Allow users to tailor frequent actions."
– Jakob Nielsen


Although this website does contain a “home page” link on the top left corner, it is very minimalist and in a font that is not consistent with the rest of their brand. I think they could’ve made it better by having a single linkable brand photo on the top left consistent throughout the rest of the pages where you can easily go back to the home page without having to look for it.



Their homepage contains two left and right side links that are represented by arrows. Once I click on the right arrow, it takes me to “Admission and Campus Life,” “Colleges and Schools,” then “Home.” Although the transition effects are cool and easy to use, this makes the site seem more messy since on the actual homepage, there are links to those other links. Making the “Admission and Campus Life” and “Colleges and Schools” have separate and distinct links isn’t really efficient in any way since those links are available with the top drop-down menu and may actually make the site function slower.



The homepage has arrows to different areas of information regarding GSU. This is all self-contained in the homepage and doesn’t require any loading or navigation other than clicking on the arrows. This helps a new user get acclimated to what GSU offers without having to navigate thoroughly. This also helps experienced users get to where they want faster if possible. They might develop a pattern or recognize how to get to their designated area through this gallery of scrolling information.
